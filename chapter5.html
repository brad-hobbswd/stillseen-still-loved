Chapter 5

Supremacy Wears Many Disguises

Recognizing inequality and remembering God's radical equality

Supremacy is quiet. Supremacy is subtle. Supremacy can look like kindness, piety, or even faith. Supremacy can come wrapped in scripture, tradition, or the insistence that one way is the right way.

Supremacy often does not arrive loudly. Supremacy slips into assumptions, comments, exclusions, and subtle moments that leave another person feeling less than, wrong, or unworthy.

Pretending your understanding of God gives authority over another person's humanity is not God's voice.
Galatians 3:28 "There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither bond nor free, there is neither male nor female: for ye are all one in Christ Jesus."

God does not elevate one soul over another. God does not create categories of worth. Radical equality remains central throughout Scripture.

Jesus repeatedly confronted systems built upon superiority. Consider the Pharisees. Guardians of the law often focused upon judgment and outward appearance while missing mercy, justice, and compassion.

Think of the woman caught in adultery. A crowd stood prepared to condemn. Jesus responded:

John 8:7 "He that is without sin among you, let him first cast a stone at her."

Those words dismantled false superiority.

Judgment frequently disguises fear. Hierarchy frequently disguises insecurity. Supremacy frequently disguises woundedness.

No person possesses authority over your worth. No crowd determines your place before God. No voice removes dignity from your life.

You are equal. You are worthy. You are loved.
